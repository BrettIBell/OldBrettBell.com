About Me

I am a sixth year M.D./Ph.D. candidate in the Medical Scientist Training Program at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine. I completed my thesis research in the laboratory of Dr. Chandan Guha in the Department of Radiation Oncology where I was awarded an NCI Ruth L. Kirschstein National Research Service Award (F30). I have defended my PhD thesis in Pathology and am now completing my clinical training here at Einstein.

My dissertation focused on how the physical characteristics of radiation can alter its' biological effects. These projects allowed me to gain experience with cutting edge radiotherapeutic strategies, including proton FLASH and carbon ion radiotherapy, while leading multi-institutional collaborations with the New York Proton Center, NASA Space Radiation Laboratory at Brookhaven National Laboratory, and Heidelberger Ionenstrahl-Therapiezentrum.

I also received a Bachelor of Arts in Biochemistry and Biology magna cum laude, with distinction in both majors, and a Master of Science in Chemistry from the University of Pennsylvania in 2018. While at Penn, I trained with Dr. Constantinos Koumenis in the Department of Radiation Oncology where I worked mainly on acute and delayed intestinal radiation toxicity using a novel image-guided focal irradiation model.
